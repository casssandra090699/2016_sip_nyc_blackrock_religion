Christianity

Christianity emerged during the Roman Empire in the 1st century C.E. The basic principles of Christianity are based on the life and teachings of Jesus. Jesus was a Jewish prophet who preached in Israel about purifying Judaism in order to estable the kingdom of God on earth. Christianity comprise of many branches and subcategories; the three most notable branches are Roman Catholicism, Eastern Orthodoxy, and Protestantism. Currently, Christianity is the most practiced religion worldwide with more than 2.18 billion Christians worldwide.
