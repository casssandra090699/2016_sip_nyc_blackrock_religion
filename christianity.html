Christianity

General

  • Similar beliefs as Judaism - the coming of Messiah and the belief in one God
  • Jesus of Nazareth was born Jewish
  • He preached a moral code of love, charity, and humility
  • Put to death in AD 30: he was seen as an agitator to rich people and to the government
  • Resurrected on the 3rd day after his death
  • This made him known as the Son of God and this belief helped spread religion throughout the Roman Empire and in Jewish communities in the Middle East
  • By 4th century AD 10% the of Roman Empire was a convert of Christianity
  • Slave, dispossessed farmers, and impoverished city dwellers found hope in this religion
  • Different denominations include Catholic Christians, Methodists, Presbyterian, Southern Baptists, and Lutheran
  • The Ten Commandments are the obligations Christians must fulfill in order to get into heaven and receive eternal life
  • Prayer is the act of communicating with God
  • The Bible is a compilation of 66 books and letters written by more than 40 authors during a period of approximately 1,500 years

The basic principles of Christianity are based on the life and teachings of Jesus. Jesus was a Jewish prophet who preached in Israel about purifying Judaism in order to estable the kingdom of God on earth. Christianity is comprise of many branches and subcategories; the three most notable branches are Roman Catholicism, Eastern Orthodoxy, and Protestantism. Currently, Christianity is the most practiced religion worldwide with more than 2.18 billion Christians worldwide.
