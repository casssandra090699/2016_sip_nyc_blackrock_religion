JUDAISM FAQ

Stuff to know.

What does the word “Torah” means?

It means a teaching or doctrine. In the narrow sense, it means specifically the Five Books of Moses, but it is more often used to mean the full body of teachings that God gave to Moses. The Torah is all the teachings that encompass Judaism. The Torah is how Jews know what they are supposed to do. The full body of the teachings of the Torah includes both the Written Torah and the Oral Torah. The basics of the Oral Torah have been recorded the Talmud. The Oral Torah contains what is necessary to understand the Written Torah. When referring to just the Five Books of Moses, many people will use the term "Chumash", which is derived from the word "chamesh," which means "five."

What does the term "Halachah" mean?

It means "gait," or "path." The Halachah is the full body of Law that mandates Jewish conduct, beliefs, and practices. It envelops Jews in everything they do. Jews therefore call it "Halachah," "The Way For Us To Go." One may say "This Halachah," in referring to a specific Law, or "The Halachah," when referring to the Law in general.

What does the word "Mitzvah" mean?

Mitzvah is a commandment. There are 613 Commandments in the Torah, and each of those Commandments has its associated Jewish law to detail the parameters of that Mitzvah. The Torah refers to the Ten Commandments as the "Ten Statements" The word mitzvah is used by the Torah to refer to all Jews are required to do. There are over 180 examples of mitzvah usage.

What is a mezuzah?

It means doorpost. The Torah commands Jews to write a certain two chapters from the Bible on a kosher piece of parchment, and place it on their doorposts. There are many intricate Jewish laws involved in this, including the precise shaping of the letters used in the writing. If there is any slight deviation in even one small part of any one letter, the entire mezuzah is not kosher. This same rule applies to tefillin and Torah scrolls. The essence of the mitzvah of mezuzah is the concept of the Oneness of God.

Why do some Jews write "G-d," instead of spelling it out?

The Halachah says that it is forbidden to deface the Name of God in any way, or to cause any possibility thereof. Some Rabbis are of the opinion that this applies to the word "God" in any language. Many therefore do not write the Name of God where someone might throw it out. This Law has nothing to do with taking God's Name in vain; that refers only to speech.

What is Kosher?

"Kosher" means properly prepared, and thus you may see the term "kosher mezuzah," or "kosher tefillin." In the specific sense, Kosher is most often used to refer to the Jewish Dietary Laws. This is a rather complex set of rules and regulations, involving both Biblical Laws and Rabbinical extensions. The Laws of Kosher involve three major aspects, labeled Determination, Preparation, and Exclusion.
